Two dates that anchor the entire Zschoche v. Balane void-instrument theory, surfaced by the 2026-05-16 posture report run.

**Fact 1 — Mary Worrick Keesey died March 17, 1988, in America.**
**Source:** doc#187 (Affidavit of Loss, 2023-02-22) — one of the three Keesey daughters states "I am one of the three (3) children of the late Mary Worrick Keesey, who died in America last March 17, 1988."
**Evidentiary tier:** testimonial (executed_filed affidavit). Death certificate would be gold-standard primary evidence; not yet in our corpus. Recommend Jonathan request from Sacramento County (where Marcia Keesey resides) or via US Social Security Death Index.

**Fact 2 — The Special Power of Attorney granted to Cesar M. de la Fuente was revoked on August 15, 2005.**
**Source:** doc#441 (Judicial Affidavit of Jonathan Paul Zschoche, executed_filed) — "...August 15, 2005, and that Cesar was made fully aware of this revocation. ... To prove that despite the prior revocation of his authority, Cesar De La Fuente maliciously sold the subject property to Defendant Gloria..."
**Evidentiary tier:** plaintiff testimonial. The actual REVOCATION INSTRUMENT (executed 2005, presumably notarized) is the primary evidence; not yet located in our corpus. Critical to find — it's the load-bearing void-instrument anchor.

**Fact 3 — Cesar N. dela Fuente died on June 21, 2017.** *(UPGRADED 2026-05-17 from testimonial to primary-evidence-grade)*
**Source:** doc#364 (Landbank of the Philippines' Comment in Civil Case 6839, filed 2018-05-17) — quoting Atty. Belen (counsel for the estate): "...plaintiff, on the ground that Cesar N. dela Fuente, administrator of state of Mary Worrick Keesey, died on June 21, 2017."
**Evidentiary tier:** PRIMARY — court-filed admission by the OPPOSING PARTY (LandBank's filing acknowledging the death). Stronger than self-testimony because LandBank had every reason to dispute and did not. Spelling: doc#364 uses **"Cesar N. dela Fuente"** (middle initial N — was earlier "M." in some sources; track both).
