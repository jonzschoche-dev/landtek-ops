**Jonathan (refined same day): "the report is a source of truth at the current status of the knowledge base, the synchronicity between the roles of Landtek and the Client and LEO. should clearly know the bottlenecks."**

So a Leo report is THREE things simultaneously:
  1. **Source of truth on the KB** — what's verified, what's inferred, what's missing
  2. **Synchronicity map** — who-owes-whom between Landtek, Client, and Leo; what's aligned, what's mismatched
  3. **Bottleneck visibility** — what's stuck, owner, age, suggested unblock action

When generating any report: lead with status + sync map + bottlenecks. Raw data is appendix.

Reports are the proof Leo understands the case. Every report Leo generates is a quality assessment of his intelligence + the underlying knowledge graph.

**What a high-quality report has:**

1. **Executive summary leads** — one paragraph that frames the entire case for someone with 60 seconds.
2. **Action-first hierarchy** — "what you should do next" appears BEFORE raw data. Not after.
3. **Narrative, not lists** — connects facts into a story. "Cesar's SPA was revoked Aug 2005 → his Sep 2019 deed to Balane is void → that's the core of 26-360."
4. **Visual hierarchy** — major headings + sub-sections + details. Not a flat table dump.
5. **Provenance visible but quiet** — `[V]`/`[C]` tags appear, but don't dominate visually.
6. **Sparse where possible, dense where critical** — top 5 entities matter, top 60 are noise.
7. **Decision-ready** — every section answers a Jonathan-question: "what is this case", "what do I do tomorrow", "what's at risk", "what's blocking us".
8. **No raw dumps** — entity lists, asset ledgers, action items go in APPENDICES, not the body.
9. **Timeline view** — when a date matters, show the date on a timeline visual.
10. **Cited but uncluttered** — sources cited inline like `[DOC 624]` not as massive quote blocks.

**What a low-quality report has** (current MWK-001 PDF is here):
- Same visual weight on every section
- 40-row asset ledger before any analysis
- 60 entity table that no one will read
- No "do this tomorrow" answer
- No narrative arc

**How to apply:**

When designing any report (PDF, Telegram, dashboard, email):
1. Start with the QUESTION the reader has when they open it. ("What's the state of MWK-001 right now?")
2. Answer it in ≤3 sentences at the top.
3. Below: 3-5 sections, each answering a sub-question.
4. Appendix at end: the raw data dumps for reference.
5. NEVER lead with tables.

The PDF redesign in deploy_111 is the first measure of this principle.
