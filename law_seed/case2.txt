Spouses Alcantara, et al. v. Brigida L. Nido (as attorney-in-fact of Revelen N. Srivastava), G.R. No. 165133 (Apr. 19, 2010, Carpio, J.). DOCTRINE — Civil Code Article 1874. "When a sale of a piece of land or any interest therein is through an agent, the authority of the latter shall be in writing; otherwise, the sale shall be void." Where the agent lacked WRITTEN authority to sell the land, the sale produced no legal effect, could not be ratified, and could not be enforced by specific performance. APPLICATION: every land conveyance executed by an agent without written authority to sell is void ab initio.
