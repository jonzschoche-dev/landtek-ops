Cosmic Lumber Corporation v. Court of Appeals and Isidro Perez, G.R. No. 114311 (Nov. 29, 1996, Bellosillo, J.). DOCTRINE — strict construction of agency authority to sell land. A power of attorney couched in general terms, or granted for one specific purpose, does NOT authorize the agent to sell unless it expressly mentions a sale or includes a sale as a necessary ingredient of the act authorized. The attorney-in-fact was empowered only to institute and file an ejectment suit; she instead sold a portion of the principal's land through a compromise agreement, acting without or in obvious excess of authority. HELD: "The sale ipso jure is consequently void. So is the compromise agreement." APPLICATION: an SPA authorizing an agent merely to NEGOTIATE the sale does not include the power to sell or convey; a deed of sale executed beyond that authority is void.
