Heirs of Teodoro Tulauan v. Manuel Mateo, et al., G.R. No. 248974 (Sept. 7, 2022, Inting, J.). DOCTRINE — imprescriptibility of an action to recover land on a void/inexistent conveyance. Civil Code Article 1410: "the action or defense for the declaration of the inexistence of a contract does not prescribe." Where reconveyance is grounded on a void or inexistent deed, the action is imprescriptible and may be brought at any time notwithstanding the lapse of years. APPLICATION: prescription and laches do not bar recovery of titles built on a void deed of sale.
