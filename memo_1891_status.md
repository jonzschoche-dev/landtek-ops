## 3. First-principles Filipino law (guidance for counsel)
- **RA 11032** (Ease of Doing Business & Efficient Government Service Delivery Act, 2018) created **ARTA** and the LGU-level **CART**, and obliges LGUs to keep a Citizen's Charter, observe processing timelines, and avoid the red-tape acts in **§21**. ARTA holds **supervisory authority** over CART compliance.
- **The complaint is supervisory, not appellate** — it attacks how the CART *operated* (composition/impartiality, notice, transparency), invoking ARTA MCs 2020-07 and 2021-08/11. The relief is a **review + corrective directive**, not a re-trial.
- **§17(d) referral is statutory, not a punt** — ARTA properly routed the matter to the agencies with primary competence: **DILG** (general supervision of LGUs) and **CSC** (RA 6713 ethics of the officials).
- **DILG's duty is the live argument.** Under **RA 7160 (LGC)** general supervision plus its EODB role, DILG must ensure the LGU's *administrative-process* compliance. The "cascade-only / not privy" stance understates that duty; referral of the *property* dispute to the Governor under **LGC §29** does **not exhaust** DILG's own continuing duty over transparency and red-tape compliance [doc:724]. **This is the point to press.**
- **CSC** holds the **RA 6713** track — the named officials' ethical/administrative liability.
- **The OP / Executive Secretary** is the supervisory-review / escalation forum over ARTA's handling (the OP Second Manifestation) [doc:1189].
- **If the forums keep deflecting**, the officials' *dereliction* itself is actionable before the **Office of the Ombudsman** (RA 6770).
