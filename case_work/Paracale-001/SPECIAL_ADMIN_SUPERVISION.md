## 6. Rule 80 frame (what "supervising this appointment" actually tracks)

*Non-authoritative — for supervision structure only; Botor owns the legal execution.*

A special administrator (Rule 80) is a **temporary** appointee to **preserve** the estate while regular administration/settlement is delayed. Supervising the appointment means tracking these gates:

1. **Petition filed** — verified estate, decedent, heirs, assets, grounds for delay, and why Alan is fit. *(Not yet done.)*
2. **Notice / hearing** — heirs and interested parties notified; opposition (expect "Ace") surfaces here.
3. **Order of appointment** — court's discretion; the Rule 78 order-of-preference is a guide, not a straitjacket, for a *special* administrator.
4. **Bond posted** (Rule 81) — conditioned on inventory + accounting. Budget for the premium.
5. **Inventory** of estate assets filed.
6. **Powers exercised** — take possession/preserve; sell only perishables; a special administrator **cannot** pay the decedent's debts and has **limited** power to dispose of realty without court leave.
7. **Accounting** to the court.

Two supervision watch-items baked into this: (i) an appointment order is **interlocutory / not appealable** — an adverse "Ace" would fight via certiorari, so expect delay; (ii) the special administrator's authority is **custodial, not proprietary** — it secures the land against encroachment (Pillar 1's real prize) but does **not** by itself hand PGC ownership. Keep client expectations matched to that.
