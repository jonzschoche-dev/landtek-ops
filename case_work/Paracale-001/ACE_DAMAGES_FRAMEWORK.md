# DAMAGES CLAIM vs. ACE — Brief of Facts & Framework
*(For estate counsel. Not a filing. **[V]** corpus-verified · **[O]** operator · **( ? )** to gather.)*

> **Who:** **Vicente de Leon Inocalla III ("Ace")** + **Elena de Leon Inocalla (Bombita)** and their group.
> **Objective:** recover the estate's economic losses + hold Ace accountable for bad-faith, oppressive conduct. **Keep this SEPARATE from, and coordinated with, the ejectment** (they recover different things — §4).

---

## 1. WHERE DAMAGES CAN BE CLAIMED — don't mix them (critical)
- **Ejectment (MeTC, Rule 70)** recovers ONLY **reasonable compensation for use/occupation of the Manila building (fair rental value) + attorney's fees + costs.** It **cannot** award moral/exemplary damages or the broader losses.
- **A separate ordinary civil action for damages (RTC)** is the vehicle for the fuller measure — **fruits Ace actually collected, exemplary + moral damages, attorney's fees for his vexatious appeals, and the Paracale-land losses.**
- ⚠ **Anti-splitting / forum-shopping caution:** do **not** claim the *same* Manila rents in both cases. Structure so the **ejectment covers the fair-rental compensation** and the **damages suit covers the distinct heads** (fruits beyond that measure, exemplary, moral, litigation cost, Paracale land). Counsel must plead to avoid *litis pendentia* / splitting a cause of action.

## 2. ACE'S WRONGFUL ACTS (civil-actionable) [V — docs 644/526 + the judgments]
1. **Took over and rented the estate's 10 apartment units for his own benefit.** Vicente Jr. "put his son (Ace)… in charge to manage and rent the Inocalla apartment"; the group occupied the units of Allan, Jesus, Casper, Melvin, Francisco (except Cipriana's & Senen's) and **collected the rents.**
2. **Possession in bad faith under a void title** — Ace's father's title (002-2011002723) was declared void (CC 13-131220 → SC G.R. 256997); Ace, as substitute, is a **possessor in bad faith** as a matter of record.
3. **Bad-faith / vexatious litigation** — pursued and **lost** the appeal (CA SP 161072) and petition (SC G.R. 256997), and filed a **Dec-2025 MR** to delay a final judgment.
4. **Harassment of the heirs** — "threatened and bullied" Senen, Marilou, Jesus each time they came to the units; conspiracy "to control Inocalla properties and eliminate Allan, Jesus, Marilou, Herbert."
5. **Occupation of Paracale estate land** (Front 1A) — depriving the estate of use.
> *Note:* the family's broader complaint also lists **criminal** charges (theft, threats, illegal firearms, murder, drugs, BIR). Those are **separate criminal complaints**, not civil damages — keep them in their own track; this framework is the **civil** money claim.

## 4. SEQUENCING WITH THE OTHER CASES
- **Ejectment (MeTC)** → recover possession + fair-rental compensation. File first / in parallel (see ejectment brief).
- **Damages suit (RTC)** → the fuller measure above. Can be filed by the **Special Administrator once appointed** (estate losses) and/or the **aggrieved heirs** (moral damages, personal). Best filed once the **void-title judgment is final** (it is) so bad-faith possession is settled.
- **13-131220** already gave ~₱100k fees on the title fight — do not re-claim that.

## 5. A RECOVERY ANGLE WORTH USING
Any damages Ace owes the estate can be **set off against Ace's distributive share** in Vicente Jr.'s branch of the estate (Art. 1078 et seq.; collation/charging a co-heir's share). ⚠ *But* this only bites **if Ace's filiation to Vicente Jr. is established** — and we are simultaneously **contesting that filiation** (dossier §6). Decide the posture: if we defeat his heirship he inherits nothing (no share to set off, but also no inheritance for him); if he is an heir, his share absorbs the damages. Counsel to reconcile the two positions.

## 6. EVIDENCE TO GATHER
- **Rental proof:** market rent per unit; tenant identities + amounts Ace collected; occupation start dates (per unit).
- **The void-title judgments** (13-131220 / SP 161072 / G.R. 256997) — establishes bad-faith possession as res judicata.
- **Harassment proof:** affidavits from the harassed heirs (Marilou, Jesus, Senen's record), the barangay **blotter (22 Aug 2025)**, any messages/threats.
- **Litigation-cost proof:** the estate's counsel billings across the appeals/MR.
- **Paracale occupation:** photos, tax decs, the DENR trespassing/tree-cutting incident (Mauro Bombita), witness statements.

## 7. CAUTIONS
- **Separate civil from criminal** — the theft/threats/firearms/murder charges belong in criminal complaints (and the assassination angle to the NBI/prosecutor), not this money suit.
- **Safety** — the Bombitas are assassination suspects; a barangay captain already feared them. Route enforcement through counsel + higher authorities, not local confrontation.
- **Standing** — estate-loss heads await the Special Administrator (or file via co-owner where allowed); moral damages are personal to each harassed heir.
- **Collectibility** — assess Ace's assets; the set-off-against-his-share angle (§5) may be the realistic recovery.
