## PART A: THE LEGAL FRAMEWORK — 7 LAYERS OF NULLITY

### Layer 1: Structural Void — Civil Code Art. 493 (Co-Ownership Prohibition)

Upon Mary Worrick Keesey's death in 1988, the three daughters inherited her entire T-4497 estate in *pro indiviso* shares. The estate was never partitioned. Under Civil Code Art. 493, **no co-owner — and no agent of co-owners — may sell, subdivide, or alienate specific physical portions of co-owned property before partition.**

**Consequence:** Every instrument from 1991 onward purporting to convey specific parcels of T-4497 is void as an unlawful premature partition, **independent of whatever authority Cesar may have held**.

**Evidence grade:** VERIFIED (doc#187, doc#284, doc#480 — affidavits stating the estate was never partitioned)

**Strategic value:** This layer bypasses the entire SPA debate. Even if Cesar had been given unlimited power to sell, he could not have legally conveyed a specific lot without prior partition. The layer is impervious to defense arguments about the SPA's scope.

---

### Layer 2: Narrow SPA — Civil Code Art. 1874 (Express Authority Required)

The **16 March 1992 Special Power of Attorney** (doc#416, consular CTC; doc#246 OCR this session) appointed Cesar M. de la Fuente for **limited purposes only**:

> "to negotiate for the sale of the above-described real estate property in favor of third persons in possession of CONTRACTS TO SELL executed by the previous administrator, MR. BEN LLAMANZARES and/or to those who have paid their down-payments or have made partial payments therefor to the latter."

**The operative verb is "negotiate," never "to sell."**

Under Civil Code Art. 1874: "When a sale of a piece of land or any interest therein is through an agent, the authority of the latter shall be in writing; otherwise, the sale shall be void." The Supreme Court (*Bautista-Spille v. Nicorp Management*, G.R. 214057, 19 October 2015) held: **Authority to sell land must be in "clear, unmistakable language"; authority to negotiate is NOT authority to sell.**

**Consequence:** Every deed Cesar executed purporting to sell or subdivide T-4497 — from 1991 to date — is void for want of authority.

**Evidence grade:** VERIFIED (doc#416 primary instrument in corpus; doc#246 full-text OCR confirmed operative clause)

**Strategic value:** This is the **kill-shot** in the Summary Judgment motion. The operative text is undisputed; the legal inference is settled law. Good faith of any buyer is legally irrelevant (*nemo dat quod non habet* — one cannot acquire in good faith what the seller had no authority to convey).

**Citation for briefing:** Civil Code Art. 1874; *Bautista-Spille v. Nicorp Management*, G.R. 214057, 19 Oct 2015.

---

### Layer 3: Implied Revocation (1999 Hoppe Letter)

In **19 February 1999**, Geraldine Keesey Hoppe (one of the three heirs) wrote de la Fuente and **transferred the attorney-in-fact power to Ida Buenaventura** [v: doc#1134, typed, legible, verified read].

This is an express withdrawal of Cesar's authority. From 1999 onward, Cesar rendered no accounting and ceased all contact with the family.

**Consequence:** Under agency law, this cessation of contact + explicit transfer extinguished whatever residual agency remained. All transactions 1999-2005 rest on a withdrawn authority.

**Evidence grade:** VERIFIED (doc#1134 primary document in corpus)

**Strategic value:** Secondary to Layer 1 and Layer 2, but establishes early withdrawal of trust — undercuts any "long-standing authority" defense.

**Consequence:** After 2007, no purchaser, transferee, or subsequent owner can claim status as an innocent purchaser for value. The irregularity was on the public record. This forecloses the good-faith defense for any post-2007 transaction.

**Evidence grade:** VERIFIED (case strategy memo cites the 2007 notice; need to locate publication record — Bicol Post or similar newspaper of general circulation)

### Layer 6: Death Extinguishment (21 June 2017)

Cesar M. de la Fuente died on **21 June 2017** [v: doc#364 (Landbank court filing, May 2018, refers to "the late Cesar M. de la Fuente"); doc#407 (Salvador Osum Judicial Affidavit states "My brother CESAR M. DE LA FUENTE died on 21 June 2017")].

Under Civil Code Art. 1919(3), **agency is extinguished by the agent's death**. Any instrument executed in Cesar's name after this date is not merely void for want of agency — it is **legally impossible**.

**Consequence:** Post-2017 transactions are void by impossibility. The **2021 issuance of T-079-2021002126 to Balane** was triggered by a 2016 deed (pre-death), but any title-issuance acts that occurred in 2021 (post-death) trace back through a dead-man's chain.

**Evidence grade:** VERIFIED via affidavits (doc#407, doc#364); primary death certificate still needed — but the affidavits are probative.

**Strategic value:** This is the **cleanest factual fulcrum** per the case strategy memo. It is corpus-anchored once Cesar's death certificate is obtained. Creates a bright-line rule: anything with 2017-death knowledge is ipso facto void.

### Layer 7a: Imprescriptibility of Nullity Actions

Because the underlying acts are **void ab initio** (not merely voidable), the action to declare them null **does not prescribe**. This is a rule of law, not a tolling argument (Civil Code Art. 1409, 1410).

**Consequence:** Time cannot cure what is structurally non-existent. The case is not barred by laches or prescription, regardless of the 30+ year history (1991-2026).

**Evidence grade:** RULE OF LAW (no evidence required; applies categorically)

---

### Layer 7b: Discovery-Rule Tolling (Prescriptive Claims)

For causes of action with their own prescriptive periods (Reconveyance, *Accion Reivindicatoria*, Accounting), the discovery rule applies: plaintiffs reside in the United States. Throughout 1991-2020, no encumbrance was annotated on T-4497 sufficient to give them notice. **Plaintiffs discovered the fraud only in 2020**, whereupon they immediately filed public notices, served formal demands, and pursued administrative escalation.

**Consequence:** Concealed fraud cannot give rise to laches. The prescriptive period (where applicable) runs from 2020, not from each underlying void act.

**Evidence grade:** FACT (doc#388 is the 18 Aug 2025 Final Demand; public notices of nullity in 2020-2026 demonstrate immediate diligence upon discovery)

### Expected Defenses (and our counters)

**Defense 1 — Torrens Indefeasibility:**  
"Our TCTs are indefeasible under PD 1529 after one year."

Counter: *Spring cannot rise higher than its source*. Indefeasibility does not attach when the originating instrument is void *ab initio*. Cite *Heirs of Gregorio Lopez v. DBP*, *Spouses Aguinaldo v. Torres*, *Mercado v. Mercado*, *Vda. de Cabrera v. CA*.

**Defense 2 — Innocent Purchaser for Value (IPV):**  
"We acted in good faith without notice."

Counter: Three-tier rebuttal:
- The absence of annotation on T-4497 was itself a red flag warranting due diligence.
- The 2007 Public Notice provides constructive notice; no IPV claim post-2007.
- For post-2017 (post-death) transactions, IPV is legally impossible (no valid grantor).

**Defense 3 — Laches / Prescription:**  
"30+ years of inaction bars recovery."

Counter: Action to declare nullity does not prescribe when the underlying act is void (*not* voidable). Laches requires knowledge or duty to know; plaintiffs had neither (US residents; fraud concealed by absence of RD annotations). Discovery rule applies: 2020 discovery → immediate action → no laches.

**Defense 4 — Want of Capacity / Authority to Sue:**  
"Jonathan has no authority to represent all three sisters."

Counter: **Guardianship-first strategy eliminates this entirely.** Once RTC Daet grants the MWK-GUARDIANSHIP petition, Jonathan is court-appointed guardian with unassailable authority.

**Defense 5 — Misjoinder / Improper Venue:**  
"You can't join 28+ defendants plus John Does in one action."

Counter: Common questions of law and fact (void *ab initio* doctrine, layered-nullity theory, fraud chain) bind all defendants. Joinder is proper under Rules. Venue is proper: property located within RTC Daet's jurisdiction.
