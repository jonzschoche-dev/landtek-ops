## Two conveyance modes — recommendation
- **Mode A — Recover-then-sell (RECOMMENDED for the pilot).** Clear the occupant, deliver vacant, sell at
  full value. Slower, but it proves the whole chain and every clean parcel will need it. Uses the estate's
  strongest levers (below).
- **Mode B — Sell subject-to-occupancy.** Sell now at a discount; buyer assumes the eviction. Faster cash,
  lower price, thin buyer pool for a 152 sqm occupied lot. Keep as fallback; not ideal for a *pilot* whose
  point is to prove the mechanism.

## Step 1 — Clear the occupant (the levers, all already built this session)
- **Possession in concept of owner:** the estate's FAAS + **30-year RPT payment trail** (Colen's statement
  §F, ₱1.086M) → the accion-publiciana/ejectment spine (see `T4497_LANDBANK_SQUATTER_LEDGER.md`).
- **Bad-faith builder (Arts. 449–451):** built without permit or owner permission → **no right of retention,
  no indemnity**; estate keeps improvements free or demolition at occupant's cost, **plus back-rent** (see
  `T4497_IMPROVEMENTS_LEVER.md`). This destroys the squatter's only leverage.
- **Remedy selection:** unlawful detainer (if occupation was by tolerance — faster MTC summary) vs. accion
  publiciana (if >1 yr / no tolerance). Confirm the occupation history to choose.
- **UDHA caveat (RA 7279):** if the occupant is genuinely underprivileged/homeless, due-process + relocation
  apply; but a titled/means-having occupant (Gaulit holds a competing tax declaration) is not UDHA-shielded.
  Confirm capacity-to-pay.
- **Any one co-owner may bring the ejectment (Art. 487)** — so this can start on Patricia's authority even
  before the guardianship, running in parallel.

## Step 2 — Rule 95 authority to sell (draft petition skeleton)
> RTC/guardianship court, Spec. Proc. 2680. **VERIFIED PETITION FOR AUTHORITY TO SELL REAL PROPERTY OF THE
> WARDS (Rule 95, Rules of Court).**
1. Petitioner is the duly appointed guardian of the property of the wards (the absent co-owner-heirs).
2. The wards own TCT **T-52537** (Lot 2-X-4-E, 152 sqm, Barangay 3, Mercedes), assessed MV ₱147,440.
3. **Benefit to the wards / necessity:** the parcel yields no income, carries accruing RPT, and was occupied
   by informal settlers; its recovery-and-sale converts a non-performing, cost-bleeding asset into cash for
   the estate and to reimburse the necessary expenses of administration (Rule 96 §8 / Art. 488).
4. Proposed terms: sale at not less than the court-approved value (BPO-supported), cash, buyer to be
   disclosed; net proceeds deposited to the guardianship for accounting.
5. **Prayer:** order authorizing the guardian to sell T-52537 on the stated terms, and to execute the deed.
> *(Rule 95 requires notice + hearing; the court fixes the minimum price. Court approval makes the buyer's
> title unimpeachable — a feature.)*
