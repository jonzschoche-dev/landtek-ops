## 3. Execution mechanics — from writ to money

CARP just compensation does **not** collect like an ordinary sheriff's levy against a private debtor. The path is:

1. **Motion for Execution** (Rule 39) filed in RTC Br. 40 by a properly substituted real-party-in-interest → court issues the **Writ of Execution**. `[V-doc376]`
2. **Writ served on Land Bank** (the Land Valuation & Landowners Compensation / Agrarian Operations unit — Ms. Nica's office is the intake point, **fssc5@landbank.com**, mobile 0998 293 7638; the CLAUDE.md "fsc5" is a one-letter miss — it is **fssc5**). `[V-doc363]`
3. **Mode of payment is fixed by RA 6657 §18: cash + bonds, at the statutory ratio.** For a lot **above 50 ha** (T-4494): **25% cash / 75% LBP bonds**; 24–50 ha: 30% cash / 70% bonds; ≤24 ha: 35% cash / 65% bonds. The heirs **cannot demand 100% cash** on the principal. `[V-doc376]` **The interest, however, is a delay-damages money obligation and is realistically collectible in cash — this is where the large cash number lives.** `[compute]`
4. **Release:** LBP issues the cash portion (manager's check / deposit to the heirs' account) and the bond portion as LBP bonds (10% face maturing yearly over 10 years, negotiable/usable for taxes, land, tuition, etc. per §18). `[V-doc376]`
5. **Sheriff's role** is limited — service and return; the actual disbursement runs through LBP's compensation process, not a public auction.
6. **Prior partial payments** already reduced three of the four lots (T-4501/4502/4503) — those were "preliminary payments through Cesar de la Fuente" and, per Yuzon (citing *Apo Fruits*), **do not excuse LBP's liability for delay/interest.** `[V-doc376]`

**Tax on the payout — `[to-obtain]`, flag to counsel/BIR:**
- **CARP transfers are tax-exempt at the transfer level — RA 6657 §66** (transactions under the Act are exempt from taxes and fees); expropriation/just-compensation proceeds are generally **not** treated as an ordinary taxable sale (no CGT/DST on the taking). Confirm current BIR treatment. `[to-obtain]`
- **Interest portion** may attract withholding/income tax — segregate principal vs. interest in any receipt. `[to-obtain]`
- **Estate posture:** MWK's estate was settled **extrajudicially in 1993 (PE-170452)**, so estate tax on MWK should be historic — but confirm no open estate-tax exposure and whether any heir has since died (which would layer a sub-estate). `[to-obtain]`
