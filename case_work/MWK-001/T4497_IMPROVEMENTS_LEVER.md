## 1. The lever in one page

**What the no-permit + no-permission fact wins the estate.** If a structure was built on Keesey land **without the owner's permission**, the builder is a **possessor in bad faith** — one "aware that there exists in his title or mode of acquisition [a] flaw which invalidates it" (Civil Code **Art. 526** `[LAW]`). A bad-faith builder is stripped of every protection the good-faith builder enjoys, and the landowner gets a menu of remedies, all favorable:

1. **Improvements for free.** "He who builds… in bad faith on the land of another **loses what is built… without right to indemnity**" (**Art. 449** `[LAW]`). The estate may simply *keep the house* and owe nothing.
2. **OR demolition at the builder's cost.** The owner "may demand the demolition of the work… **at the expense of the person who built**" (**Art. 450** `[LAW]`).
3. **OR compel the builder to buy the land** at its price (**Art. 450** `[LAW]`) — a forced sale on the estate's terms.
4. **Plus damages** in every case (**Art. 451** `[LAW]`).
5. **The builder recovers almost nothing** — only "the **necessary expenses of preservation of the land**" (**Art. 452** `[LAW]`), never the value of the building, and never useful or luxury improvements (**Arts. 546, 548** apply only to good-faith possessors).

**Why it is decisive — it destroys the squatter's single best defense.** A *good-faith* builder (Art. 448 + 546) has (i) a right to **reimbursement** of the improvement's value and, critically, (ii) a **right of retention** — he may **stay on the land, rent-free, until he is paid** for the building. That retention right is what lets an adverse holder sit for years and extract a buy-out. **Bad faith annihilates it:** no reimbursement, no retention (Arts. 449, 452). *This one fact — no owner permission — converts the occupant from "pay me for my house before I leave" into "leave, and either the house is mine free or you demolish it at your own cost, and you owe me damages and back-rent."* It is the fact that turns a defensive recovery suit into an offensive one.

**The no-permit fact is the second barrel.** Construction without a building permit is independently illegal under **PD 1096 (National Building Code), Sec. 301** `[LAW]`, and gives the estate an **administrative demolition route through the Office of the Building Official (OBO)** that runs *parallel to and independent of* the civil courts. It is also the single best *corroboration* of bad faith: a builder who genuinely believed he owned the land would have pulled a permit; skipping it evidences knowledge of the defect (feeds the Art. 526 bad-faith finding).

**Where it plugs in:** it super-charges RENT (the attornment ultimatum becomes "sign a lease, or lose the house for free *and* pay to demolish it *and* owe damages"), RECOVERY (Arts. 449–451 + damages fold straight into the reivindicatoria/publiciana prayer and the de la Fuente unified-nullity complaint, doc 959), and MONETIZATION (a recovered bad-faith building becomes estate rental stock or added sale value at **zero acquisition cost**).

### 0.1 The honest tension you must hold (do not skip)

The corpus already shows that, for at least the **titled** adverse holders, **building permits were NOT skipped — they were *issued* (on void titles).** The de la Fuente unified-nullity complaint pleads that officials "**Knowingly allowed the issuance of building permits and property clearances based on these void titles, actively facilitating physical encroachment**" `[V]` doc 959. So a blanket "no building permit exists" claim is **wrong for the Balane/Tortalha subdivision** and must not be asserted there. The lever splits cleanly by occupant type (§2):

- **Informal / tolerated occupants (e.g. Antonio Teope)** → the **"no permit"** theory is live; the estate has already sought a *Certificate of No Record* for exactly this structure `[V]` doc 772.
- **Titled adverse holders (Balane, Tortalha)** → permits likely **issued but VOID/irregular** — issued on a void title, and the PD 1096 §301 "proof of ownership / right to build" requirement could not have been lawfully satisfied by a void-title holder. Here the lever is *"permit void + facilitation of encroachment"* (an OBO-officer / ombudsman angle), **not** "no permit." The **no-permission → bad-faith-builder** analysis still applies to them in full once their titles are nullified.

## 5. Honest caveats

1. **Bad faith is a legal conclusion that must be PROVEN, and good faith is presumed.** Art. 527 `[LAW]`: "**Good faith is always presumed, and upon him who alleges bad faith… rests the burden of proof.**" The operator's "no permission" assertion `[OP]` is the raw material; it becomes an Art. 526 finding only on evidence (§3 items 1, 2). **Do not plead or represent "bad-faith builder" as established** — plead it as a count to be proven, subsidiary to the ownership claim.
2. **The Art. 453 both-in-bad-faith / tolerance trap.** Art. 453 `[LAW]`: if **both** parties acted in bad faith, they are treated as if **both** in good faith — which would *revive* the builder's reimbursement + retention right and gut the lever. Art. 453 ¶2: "**the [landowner] is in bad faith [if] the act was done with his knowledge and without opposition.**" **The squatters' best counter is estate tolerance** — "the heirs knew and did nothing for years." The estate must **rebut tolerance affirmatively**, and it can: 30-year continuous **RPT payment**, the **2005 SPA revocation** (doc 76/79), the **2023 amnesty settlement** (doc 290), and the **suits themselves** (Civil Case 26-360, the ARTA/records demands, the FOI to the OBO) all evidence *owner opposition*, not acquiescence. Marshal these to defeat Art. 453 — it is the pivotal factual fight of this lever.
3. **UDHA due-process / relocation constraints.** For any occupant who is *genuinely* underprivileged-and-homeless, RA 7279 Secs. 28–30 require due process, notice, and (often) relocation before eviction/demolition. The lever's summary teeth apply only to *professional* squatters / titled holders with means — hence evidence #4 is not optional.
4. **Demolition is never self-help.** Neither Art. 450 nor PD 1096 authorizes the estate to knock anything down itself. Demolition requires a **court order** (civil) or an **OBO/LGU abatement order with its own due process** (administrative). Any physical removal without an order is itself unlawful and would hand the occupant a counterclaim. Every demolition path here is *apply for an order*, not *act*.
5. **The "no permit" fact is occupant-specific, not estate-wide (§0.1).** Corpus evidence (doc 959) shows permits *were issued* for the titled subdivision. Asserting "no building permit exists" across the estate would be false and impeachable. Scope the no-permit claim to the informal structures (Teope) where a Certificate of No Record is actually sought.
6. **No structure is documented yet.** The corpus contains **zero** proven structure inventory — no building tax-dec entry, photo, or OBO record in hand (the improvement records are *withheld*, doc 2388). The entire per-parcel table §2 rests on the operator's assertion plus occupant names; it is a remedy map, not proof. Everything upstream of the §3 evidence pack is `[OP]`/`[U]`.
