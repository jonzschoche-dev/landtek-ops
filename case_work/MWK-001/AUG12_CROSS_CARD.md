## The 3 tells that can sink you — kill them
1. **The hedge.** No *"it appears / in my opinion / we believe."* You either know it, or you point to the document. Delete these words.
2. **Answering the question you *wish* they asked.** Answer THEIR question in one clean line first — *then* reframe. Don't volunteer.
3. **Over-denial.** Concede every true thing instantly: **he WAS the agent** (limited); the receipts **WERE** signed (by Salvador); the typo **IS** there. Deny only the false part. Conceding small truths is what makes your "no" on the void sale bulletproof.

## Golden rules
- **Facts over argument.** Her affidavit beats your opinion. Law and pleadings → *"that's for counsel."*
- **Documents over memory.** *"I won't guess at survey lots — the certified titles prove it."*
- **Two sentences, then stop.** Silence is not your enemy.
- Concede the tiny true things *cheerfully*; be immovable on the class limitation.
