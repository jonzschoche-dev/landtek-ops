**The LGU's own admission it never acquired the land — the crown fact.** **Resolution No. 76-96** (Sangguniang Bayan of Mercedes, 10th Regular Session, **13 Mar 1996**) creates an "AD HOC COMMITTEE TO TREAT MATTER WITH THE PROPOSED LEGAL ACQUISITION/DONATING OF PARCEL OF LAND OWNED BY MARY WORRICK KEESEY ESTATE," reciting that the Municipal Hall, Municipal Health Center, and Bureau of Fisheries "are presently occupying" the estate's land and that a donation was still merely "proposed" `[V]` doc 389 (verbatim). **In 1996 the LGU admitted, on its own record, that the land it sits on is the Keesey estate's and that acquisition had not happened.** The 1953 "deed of donation" (doc 279) is the void instrument (no perfected/registered donation; the matter pleads "no signed instrument; council admits none") — Civil Code Art. 749 requires a public instrument for a land donation to be valid `[LAW]`.

**The doctrine.** Where government takes/occupies private land without expropriation or payment, the owner's constitutional right to **just compensation** (1987 Const. Art. III §9) is enforced through an action for **inverse condemnation** — the owner compels the taker to pay. The State's **non-suability does NOT bar it**: by taking private property for public use without paying, the government is deemed to have waived immunity (the *Amigable v. Cuenca* / *Ministerio v. CFI* / *Republic v. Sandiganbayan* line — verify the current controlling G.R. before counsel relies) `[LAW][HV]`. The owner recovers **just compensation for the land + reasonable rental/interest for the period of occupation**.

**Quantifying the estate exposure:**
- **Just-compensation floor (land value):** **₱10.4M `[V]`** at current assessor MV. **Honest caveat:** just compensation is legally valued **at the time of taking**, not today — a 1950s–90s taking values the *principal* far lower. But, exactly as in CV6839, the **interest from the taking date to payment** (6–12% p.a. over ~30 years) balloons the collectible; the ₱10.4M current MV is the defensible *floor* to state, with the taking-date valuation + interest computation flagged to counsel.
- **Reasonable accrued rental (alternative/cumulative measure):** at the rent-pathway's residential basis of **~0.5%/mo of MV = ~₱52,000/mo `[EST]`**, over the documented occupation period **1996 → 2026 (~30 yrs)**: **≈ ₱18.7M `[EST]`** accrued (₱52k × 360 mo). Conservative/mid band **₱15–19M `[EST]`** depending on the proven taking date. **This is a notional rental, not a comp — never quote outward as fact.**
- **Front-1 estate-revenue envelope: ₱10.4M `[V]` floor → ~₱25–29M `[EST]`** with rental/interest.

**Which parcels / occupying use / admission docs:** PINs …-005-01-007/008/025 (T-32911) → Municipal Hall + Health Center + Bureau of Fisheries + barangay hall + road + Jimmy P. Lo Multi-Purpose Center. ## 4. FRONT 3 — Back-rent from bad-faith builders/occupants

### 4.1 The claim + per-occupant table

**Legal basis.** A possessor who built/occupied **without the owner's consent** is a **bad-faith possessor** (Art. 526) and owes **reasonable compensation for use and occupation** for the occupation period, plus (Arts. 449–451) loses improvements / faces demolition-at-cost + **damages** `[LAW]`. Back-rent is pleaded as a **subsidiary count folded into each recovery/ejectment prayer** — it is not a stand-alone suit (that is why it is the fastest-to-cash of the three: the same judgment that recovers the land awards the back-rent).

**Basis for the numbers:** reasonable rent = **~0.5%/mo of assessor MV** (the rent-pathway residential basis, `[EST]`, never a comp). Back-rent presupposes **proving occupant + bad faith + occupation-start date** (see gate).
