# Squatter Engagement & Rights-Assertion Scheme — MWK Estate (2026-07-19)

**Goal:** engage the occupants toward revenue (they pay, they buy, or they leave) while — in the SAME act —
asserting and preserving every ownership right and building the ejectment case. No engagement may ever read
as tolerance, waiver, or recognition of the occupant's claim. **Internal scheme for counsel (Botor); every
outward communication is human-gated — nothing sent by the system.**

## The core idea
The occupants are the **ready market.** Facing ejectment + demolition-at-their-cost + back-rent (bad-faith
builder, Arts. 449–451) with **no right of retention**, most rational occupants will **regularize (pay rent
or buy)** rather than lose everything. So the estate monetizes the occupation itself — but only through an
instrument that **simultaneously offers and asserts.**

## Segment the occupants FIRST (census — the prerequisite, gap G-1)
| Tier | Who | Engagement |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Informal squatters** (no title) | e.g. the Gaulit-type competing tax-declarant on T-52537; unknown occupants on the Barangay-1/3 lots | **ENGAGE** with the demand+offer+reservation letter (below) — lease/buy/vacate |
| **2. Titled adverse holders** (Balane + the 7 transferees) | hold derivative TCTs off the void de la Fuente chain | **DO NOT offer a lease** (it would concede their title) → recovery/nullity track + demand to reconvey |

A one-pass barangay canvass + assessor-roll cross-check assigns each occupant a tier. **You cannot engage
what you haven't named.**

## The engage-and-assert doctrine (why one act does both)
1. **Attornment / tenant-estoppel** — a lessee is estopped from denying the lessor's title (Rule 131 §2(b)).
   The moment an occupant signs a lease or pays rent, they **acknowledge the estate's ownership** and can
   **never claim adverse possession.** Engaging them as a tenant *destroys* their prescription claim.
2. **Tolerance → unlawful-detainer predicate** — frame the current occupation as **by the estate's mere
   tolerance, now withdrawn.** A written **demand to pay and vacate** is the jurisdictional predicate for a
   fast MTC unlawful-detainer suit (Rule 70). So the engagement letter *is* the first step of the ejectment.
3. **Reservation of rights** — every offer is made **"without prejudice to and with express reservation of
   all the estate's rights, and without recognition of any right of the occupant."** No waiver, no estoppel
   against the owner.
4. **Registered rights-act, paired with the engagement** — annotate a **Notice of Adverse Claim (PD 1529
   §70)** / **Lis Pendens (§76)** on the contested titles and file the estate's tax-dec/RPT record. This
   makes the assertion durable + public (constructive notice), so no third party can claim good faith and no
   engagement can be spun as acquiescence.

## The offer ladder (what each occupant is offered)
1. **Regularize — LEASE** (informal occupants): sign a ≤1-yr renewable lease, pay rent → immediate income +
   attornment (kills their claim). *(Lease authority: guardian post-07-27, or co-owner majority.)*
2. **Regularize — BUY**: purchase the lot at a fair (BPO) price → the estate's fastest sale (the occupant is
   the motivated buyer; no need to source one or deliver vacant). *(Sale authority: Rule 95 guardian.)*
3. **VACATE** peacefully within a set period → estate recovers, then sells clean.
4. **REFUSE** → unlawful detainer / accion publiciana + **back-rent** + demolition at their cost (bad-faith
   builder, no indemnity). The stick that makes 1–3 the rational choice.

> 1. The estate is the **registered and taxpaying owner** (TCT ___; tax dec ARP ___; RPT paid 19__–2026).
>    You occupy **without title or right**, heretofore **by the owner's mere tolerance, which is now
>    withdrawn.**
> 2. **Demand:** within [15] days, **either** (a) execute a lease and pay reasonable rent of ₱___/mo and
>    accrued back-rent; (b) purchase the lot at ₱___; **or** (c) peacefully vacate and remove nothing that
>    accedes to the land.
> 3. Any structure was built **without the owner's consent and without a building permit** → under Arts.
>    449–451 you have **no right to reimbursement or retention**; on default the owner may recover the
>    improvement or have it demolished **at your expense**, plus damages and back-rent.
> 4. **This offer is made without prejudice to, and with express reservation of, all the estate's rights,
>    and without any recognition of a right in you.** Non-compliance = suit for unlawful detainer / recovery.

## Guardrails (do not skip)
- **UDHA (RA 7279):** if an occupant is genuinely **underprivileged and homeless**, due-process +
  relocation apply before eviction/demolition; professional squatters / those with means (a competing tax
  dec, capacity to pay) are **not** shielded. Screen capacity-to-pay per occupant.
- **Never offer a lease to a Tier-2 titled adverse holder** — that concedes their title. They get a demand
  to reconvey + the nullity track.
- **Authority:** the **demand + ejectment** can start NOW on any one co-owner (Art. 487 — Patricia's SPA),
  in parallel with the guardianship; **leases and sales** need the guardian (Rule 95) / co-owner majority.
- **Outward chokepoint:** every letter/annotation is **drafted internally and sent only on the operator's
  express go**, through counsel. Nothing auto-sends.

## Rollout sequence
1. **Census + tier** each occupied parcel (barangay canvass + assessor-roll cross-check). Start T-52537
   (Gaulit?) as the pilot.
2. **Register the rights-act** (adverse claim / lis pendens) on the contested titles — the durable assertion.
3. **Serve the demand+offer+reservation** letter per Tier-1 occupant (human-gated).
4. **Bank the acceptances** (lease income / sale) → attornment kills their claims; **escalate refusals** to
   unlawful detainer + back-rent.
5. **Tier-2/3** run their own tracks (nullity / inverse condemnation) in parallel.

## Why this is the whole occupied-estate engine
It fuses the fronts already built: **rent pathway** (lease income), **recovery/MWK-DLF-VOID** (ejectment/
nullity), **improvements lever** (bad-faith stick), **back-rent** (O3), **tax-dec possession** (the proof),
and **monetization** (buy option = the fastest sale). One instrument, applied per occupant, turns the
occupied liability into rent, sales, and cleared land — while locking down the estate's rights at every step.
