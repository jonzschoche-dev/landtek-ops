# Aug-12 cross-examination rehearsal — Jonathan (Patricia's witness, CV 26-360)

Drill pack built from spine §10 + the 2026-07-18 evidence-posture audit (§6). Every attack line below
is anchored in the defendants' actual filings (Answer doc 405, Salvador JA doc 407, Balane JA doc 1089)
or in our own papers' documented soft spots. **Approved answers ≤2 sentences. Concede small, pivot to
the class limitation. "The document says" beats memory, every time.**

## The golden rules (say them until they're reflex)
1. **"Revoked 2005 — published 2020 — annotated 2021."** In that order, every time. Never imply 2005 publication.
2. **Never argue "he couldn't sell at all."** The SPA authorized a sale — **to Llamanzares Contract-to-Sell
   holders only**. Mrs. Balane was never one. That's the whole case; good faith is irrelevant to a void sale.
3. **Own the typos calmly.** The deed and the title govern, not a clerical slip in an affidavit.
4. **Never claim personal knowledge you don't have** (1992 signing, 2005 notice to Cesar). You testify as
   attorney-in-fact **on the documents and family records**.
5. Two sentences, then stop. Silence is not your enemy.

## The eight attack lines — and the approved answer

### 1. Personal knowledge ("You weren't there, were you?")
*"You were not present when the 1992 SPA was signed. You have no personal knowledge of what the family told Cesar. Everything you know, someone told you or you read."*
**Answer:** "I testify as Patricia's attorney-in-fact on the family's documents and records — the SPA, the revocation instrument, the titles, the deeds. Those documents are in evidence and they say what they say."

### 2. The SPA ¶7 reading (their best punch — from Answer ¶39)
*"The SPA authorizes de la Fuente to 'execute deeds' and 'receive proceeds.' So he COULD sell. Your whole theory is wrong, isn't it?"*
**Answer (concede-and-pivot, per the counsel memo):** "Yes — the SPA authorizes sales, **to a defined class: the buyers already holding Ben Llamanzares Contracts-to-Sell**. Mrs. Balane never held one — she bought 'rights' from Kenneth Asistin, not from the estate." *(Never defend negotiate-only. If shown your own JA Q53, concede the wording and restate the class limitation.)*

### 3. The 2005-notice attack (soft spot §6.10 — do NOT overclaim)
*"Show me one document proving Cesar was NOTIFIED of this revocation in 2005. There is none, correct? Salvador says the family never told him."*
**Answer:** "The 2005 instrument itself disowned every SPA other than Ida Buenaventura's; it was published in 2020 and annotated in 2021 — the documents show those dates. Whether Cesar was personally told doesn't change that the 2016 sale was outside the SPA's class of authorized buyers in the first place." *(Do NOT say "we informed him in 2005" — no document backs it and Salvador denies it, doc 407 S32.)*

### 4. The typo nitpicks (§6.9 — they WILL use these)
*"Your sworn affidavit says the sale was September 26, 2019. Wrong by three years, under oath. Your affidavit says 28,981 sqm; the complaint says 28,891. Which of your sworn statements is false?"*
**Answer:** "A clerical error in the statement of purposes — the affidavit's own Q35 and the deed itself say September 2016, and the title says 28,891 square meters. The documents govern; I stand by them." *(Calm, once, no elaboration. If pressed again: "As I said — the deed and the certificate of title control.")*

### 5. The receipts attack ("your family DID get money")
*"Exhibit 5 — receipts your family's side issued. You claim the heirs never received a peso, yet here are the receipts."*
**Answer:** "The earliest of those receipts is October 2003, they're signed by Salvador — 'Von' — who was never the attorney-in-fact, none of them names a Llamanzares contract, and **not one of them is for the 2016 Balane sale**. Patricia received nothing from that sale."

### 6. The equities ("50 years, taxes, improvements")
*"The Balanes have lived there since 1975 — fifty years. Paid taxes. Built their home. And your family, in America, never once objected."*
**Answer:** "Mrs. Balane's own affidavit says she bought 'rights and possession' from Kenneth Asistin for ₱16,500 and **paid the owners' side ₱50 a month rent** — a renter acknowledges whose land it is. Occupancy and improvements don't convert a void deed into a valid one."

### 7. The chain probe (§10c — the T-32917 trap)
*"Walk me through how T-52540 connects to your mother title. There's a gap, isn't there? It came directly from T-32917?"*
**Answer (anchor):** "T-52540 covered this exact parcel — Lot 2-X-6-I-4-C, 2,587 sqm — and it stood **in the name of co-owner Geraldine Hoppe**; that's the certified copy, and the de la Fuente side's own 2017 Deed of Confirmation says the same." **If pushed on ancestry:** "The immediate parent is T-49061 — Lot 2-X-6-I-4, segregated under plan Psd-05-026197 — and the root lot is 2-X-6, title T-32917. That's on the register." *(Do NOT accept "directly from T-32917." The correction shows command of the record.)*

### 8. NEW — Cesar's death (now answerable)
*"Where is Cesar de la Fuente in all this? Wouldn't HE be the person to explain his authority?"*
**Answer:** "Mr. de la Fuente died on June 21, 2017 — his own counsel told the court so in the DAR case. That's why the accounting we seek runs against his estate, and why everything registered in 2021 was necessarily done by others." *(Precision guard: his death does NOT void the 2016 deed — never claim it does; the deed fails for the class limitation, not the death.)*
