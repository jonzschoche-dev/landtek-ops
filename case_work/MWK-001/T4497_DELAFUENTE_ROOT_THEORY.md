## 4. One master action vs eight target-by-target

**Recommendation: ONE consolidated action** — a Complaint for **Declaration of Nullity of the 1992
SPA-derived deeds + Reconveyance + Cancellation of the derivative titles**, joining all provable
de la Fuente buyers, **but staged behind the 26-360 ruling and built only on `[V]` deeds.** Reasoning:

- **Common question (permissive joinder, Rule 3 §6).** Every deed carries the *same recital, the same
  agent, the same limited-object 1992 SPA*. The nullity of that authority is a **question of law and
  fact common to all** — the textbook basis for joining defendants in one suit. One court, one ruling
  on the SPA, binds the whole layer; eight separate suits risk **inconsistent rulings on the identical
  instrument** (one MTC voids the SPA, another upholds it) — the worst outcome for the estate.
- **Efficiency + pattern force.** Presented together, the deeds prove a *campaign* (serial self-dealing
  by a fiduciary), which is far more persuasive on fraud/bad-faith than any single sale. The
  below-value pricing pattern (§1e) only reads as fraud in aggregate.
- **Prescription — imprescriptible.** An action to declare a **void** deed and to reconvey property
  held on an implied/constructive trust does not prescribe (Civil Code Art. 1410; the void-contract
  and *nemo dat* line). This holds for the pre-2005 sales (Nacasa/Victa/Vela) as much as Balane. The
  clock only bites if a defect is *voidable* (fraud, 4 yrs) or if a buyer is an innocent purchaser
  under a Torrens title indefeasible after 1 year — which is precisely why **V1 (void, not voidable)
  must lead** and why each buyer's good faith must be defeated (agent-dealing duty of inquiry).

**But three hard constraints force staging, not a big-bang filing:**

1. **Forum / jurisdiction is split by parcel value.** Jurisdiction over a real action is set by the
   **assessed value of each parcel** (RA 11576). Balane's parcel and the small lots sit in **MTC**
   (assessed value < ₱400,000). But the aggregate, or a larger parcel (Pascual's two titles, the
   T-4497-root holdings), **could exceed the RTC threshold**. A single consolidated action may not fit
   one court's jurisdiction — **you cannot aggregate assessed values across distinct parcels to force
   RTC, nor split a void-SPA theory across two courts without risking inconsistent SPA rulings.**
   → **Pull the assessed value of every target parcel first** (§2 worklist); jurisdiction dictates
   whether this is one RTC action or a lead MTC action + joined smaller ones.

2. **Balane-gating / blowback.** 26-360 is live and near ruling (SJ + Aug-12). **Filing an estate-wide
   nullity action now would (a) put the shared 1992-SPA theory before a second court that could rule
   *against* the SPA-invalidity before MTC Mercedes rules *for* it — handing the Balane defense an
   adverse precedent to import; and (b) invite consolidation/abatement motions that stall 26-360.**
   → **Do not file the master action until 26-360's SJ/JA is resolved.** A favorable 26-360 judgment
   voiding the *same* 1992 SPA becomes the master action's cornerstone precedent.

3. **Evidence readiness is uneven.** Only Balane is trial-ready. Filing the master action against
   unread-deed defendants (Valledor/Santiago/Tychingco/Vergara) on inference invites dismissal and
   sanctions, and a mis-joined non-Keesey parcel (T-33416) is a defense gift.

**Net sequencing (blowback-aware):**
- **Now (diligence, no filing):** read doc 369 + heightened-OCR the doc 590 Ramirez/Pascual/Vela/Victa
  portions → convert 4–5 holders to `[V]`; pull assessed values + current-owner traces for the four
  unread targets and the cancelled titles.
- **Feed 26-360:** the Hansol (413), Nacasa/Victa/Vela/Pascual (590) deeds are **pattern evidence of a
  campaign** admissible *inside* 26-360 to defeat Balane's good faith and show the void SPA was used
  serially — **use them there first** (they help 26-360 without opening a new forum).
- **After a favorable 26-360 ruling:** file the **one consolidated nullity/reconveyance action** in the
  correct forum(s) by parcel value, joining the `[V]`-proven de la Fuente buyers, with the 26-360
  judgment as the anchor precedent on the void SPA.

## 5. De la Fuente as double agent (fraud / breach of trust — leads only, human-gated)

The estate's own tax declarations (2011/2014 series) carry the **Administrator line "C/O Cesar de la
Fuente"** (`[V]` ledger doc 30) — i.e., he held himself out as the estate's caretaker on the very
parcels he was selling to strangers. His own 2012 SPA (doc 38) states his mandate was to *"exercise
general control and supervision over all real estate properties … prevent by all lawful means the
commission of any trespass or waste … and to sue for the protection against any such damages."* He was
the guardian of the henhouse.

**The breach, stacked:**
1. **Self-dealing against the object.** A limited-object agent (sell only to Llamanzares CTS holders,
   to pay the estate's taxes) instead sold serially to tenants, relatives, and strangers.
2. **Proceeds never reached the purpose.** The SPA's stated purpose was to clear the estate's unpaid
   RPT; the estate still owed **₱775,202** in 2023 (`[V]` doc 290). Where did ~₱450k+ of aggregate sale
   proceeds (Balane 250k + Nacasa 92k + Victa 31k + Hansol 74k + …) go? **Conversion / failure to
   account** — the accounting defendant is now **Cesar's estate** (he died 21 Jun 2017, doc 673).
3. **Grossly-below-value sales** (~10% of assessed MV, §1e) — the classic fiduciary-fraud fingerprint.
4. **Void sub-delegation.** In 2012 he purported to **delegate his agency to Salvador O. dela Fuente**
   (doc 38) "in view of my … advanced age." An agent may not appoint a substitute unless authorized by
   the principal (Civil Code Art. 1892); if the 1992 SPA gave no such power, **the Salvador sub-agency
   is itself void**, and the entire post-2012 "Salvador" cascade (including the 2021 title issuances,
   which ran *after* Cesar's 2017 death) rests on nothing.

**Cross-references (leads, NOT filed — filing against any officer is human-gated):**

  post-2012 acts, **Salvador O. dela Fuente** — folds into the master action (§4) as the constructive-

- **Criminal (estafa, RPC Art. 315 1(b) — misappropriation by an agent):** a fiduciary who received
  sale proceeds under a duty to apply them to the estate's taxes and instead converted them fits
  estafa-by-conversion. **Prescription is the gate** (estafa prescribes; most sales are 1999–2016) and
