## 0. The crux — the SPA was legit; the fight is WHEN they were fired

We do **not** dispute that the 1992 Munda SPA was a genuine, validly executed instrument. The
decisive question is the **termination date of the agency** — because every de la Fuente conveyance
*after* termination is void for want of authority, no matter how real the SPA once was.

Three candidate termination dates, earliest to latest — each sweeps in more transactions:

| Termination date | Basis | What it voids |
|---|---|---|
| **1995** (principal's strongest line) | Last act taken under the SPA = 17-Apr-1995; the de la Fuentes' **own tax-payment records stop in 1995** (the PDF Von handed Jonathan — to be ingested); doc:1011 ("revoked as early as 1995") | Everything from the 2000 & 2003 confirmations onward |
| **15-Aug-2005** (the hard legal ceiling) | The heirs' **registered Notice of Revocation** (doc:76): "revoked on 15 August 2005… any and all transactions… after said date were unauthorized and, therefore, VOID." Registration = constructive notice to all third parties (Art. 1921) | The **2016 Balane deed**, 2018 Iligan sale, 2021 confirmation |
| **Cesar's death (2016–2020)** | Agency is extinguished by the agent's death by operation of law (Art. 1919). Cesar executed the Balane deed 29-Sep-2016 (doc:446) and was "the late Mr. De La Fuente" by 2020 (doc:76) | The 2021 confirmation (by Salvador) — void regardless |

**Why 2005 already wins the Balane parcel:** a *registered* revocation binds everyone — no buyer can
claim good-faith reliance on an authority publicly cancelled 11 years before the 29-Sep-2016 deed.
The defendants must prove the agency *survived* past 2005; the registered notice forecloses it.
**The 1995 line (boosted by Von's tax PDF) is the stronger reach** that also voids the 2000/2003 acts.

## 4. Philippine law supporting recovery (scaffolding for counsel — confirm citations)

**A0. Termination of the agency (the crux).**
- **Art. 1919** — agency is extinguished by, among others, **revocation** by the principal and the
  **death of the agent.** Cesar's death thus terminated it by operation of law (latest bound).
- **Art. 1920** — the principal may revoke the agency **at will.** The heirs did so on 15-Aug-2005.
- **Art. 1921** — where the agency was to deal with specified persons, revocation **registered**/
  given notice **binds those persons.** The Notice of Revocation was registered → constructive
  notice to all who dealt with the title after 15-Aug-2005. No good-faith reliance survives it.
- **Art. 1922** — as to third persons who in good faith dealt with the agent **without notice** of
  revocation, the agency subsists *until they learn of it.* Registration supplies that notice — so
  this defense is closed for any post-2005 buyer; and the 1995 line cuts even earlier.
- Effect: once termination is fixed, every later "deed of sale"/"confirmation of sale" by Cesar **or**
  Salvador is an act of a non-agent → falls into the void/unauthorized analysis below.

**A. The sales are VOID for want of authority to convey.**
- **Civil Code Art. 1874** — "When a sale of a piece of land… is through an agent, the authority of
  the latter shall be **in writing; otherwise the sale shall be void.**" The written SPA authorized
  *negotiation*, not sale → the deeds are void.
- **Art. 1878(5)** — a *special* power is required "to enter into any contract by which the ownership
  of an immovable is transmitted." A power to *negotiate* is not a power to *transmit ownership*.
- **Art. 1879** — special powers are **strictly construed**; "a special power to sell excludes…" —
  authority is not expanded by implication. ("Negotiate" ≠ "sell.")
- Jurisprudence (to confirm): *Cosmic Lumber Corp. v. CA* (agent's authority strictly construed; sale
  beyond authority is void); *Dizon v. CA*; *Yoshizaki v. Joy Training*; *San Juan Structural v. CA*.

**B. A void deed transmits no title; the derivative Torrens titles fall with it.**
- **Art. 1409 / 1410** — void/inexistent contracts produce no effect and the action to declare them
  void **does not prescribe**. A Torrens title (079-2021002126 etc.) springing from a void deed is
  itself void and may be cancelled; reconveyance on a void contract is **imprescriptible**, and is
  further imprescriptible while the true owner remains in possession.
- **Art. 1317 / 1403(1)** — a contract entered in the principals' name without authority is
  unenforceable and **cannot bind them absent ratification.**

**C. No ratification — because the principal never received a peso.**
- Ratification requires the principal to **knowingly accept the benefits.** Patricia received **nothing**
  → no ratification, no estoppel, no implied agency. This single fact rebuts the defendants' likely
  "she benefited / acquiesced" defense.
- **Art. 1891** — an agent must **account for and deliver to the principal everything received.**
  Cesar/Salvador received the price and **never remitted** → breach of the agency's core fiduciary duty.
- **Art. 22 (unjust enrichment) / Art. 1456 (constructive trust)** — whoever holds title/proceeds from
  the void sales holds them in trust for the heirs; supports **accounting + reconveyance + damages.**

**D. The remedy already pleaded fits.**
- Civil Case 26-360 is exactly this: *accion reivindicatoria + recovery of possession + declaration of
  nullity of the deed of absolute sale + cancellation of TCT + ARP cancellation + accounting + damages.*
  The analysis above is the **engine** for it — and it is **estate-wide within the T-32917 branch**,
  not Balane-specific (see the coverage map). Each other de la Fuente-era sub-lot is recoverable on
  the *same* void-authority theory + the RD "no record on file" certification (doc:353).
