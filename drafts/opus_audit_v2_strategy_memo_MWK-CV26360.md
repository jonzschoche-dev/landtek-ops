## (B) What's Still Wrong

**1. The Cesar death date is asserted as fact in two places with no source flagged.**
§I says "his death in 2017." §II Layer 6 says "21 June 2017." §IV-Cause D treats Mary's death certificate as primary-needed but does NOT flag Cesar's death certificate the same way — and then §VI item 8 finally admits it must still be obtained. The memo cannot simultaneously call Layer 6 "the cleanest factual fulcrum" and not have the death certificate in hand. Either Layer 6 is provisional until the cert is authenticated, or the cert acquisition is item #1 on the next-30-days list. Right now it's item #8 in §VI and missing from §VII entirely.

**2. The 2005 revocation problem is acknowledged but not resolved at the theory level.**
§I's recommendation says "conditional on locating the 2005 formal revocation primary instrument (or formally substituting it with affidavit testimony)." But §II Layer 4 still asserts the 2005 revocation as a load-bearing layer ("unquestionably void"). If the instrument is testimonial-only, Layer 4 cannot carry "unquestionably." The theory section needs to be written to survive the worst-case evidentiary outcome — which §VIII actually concedes ("primary instruments cannot be located … testimonial-heavy fraud action"). Theory and risk posture are inconsistent.

**3. The 35-40 derivative title count is asserted in §I and §VI and never sourced.**
The corpus maps 26 verified/inferred (§IV-Cause B). The complaint claims 35-40. §VII Item 4 asks Leo to "locate the remaining 9-14." This is backwards — a partner does not want to see a complaint claim a number 35% larger than what the evidence inventory supports. Either plead what you can prove and amend, or close the gap before filing. The current posture invites a Rule 8 specificity challenge.

**4. Layer 7 (laches/discovery) cites no authority and conflates two doctrines.**
"Plaintiffs reside in the United States" is not a legal basis for tolling. The argument should be (a) void-act-imprescriptibility (a rule, not a discovery argument) and (b) concealed-fraud discovery rule (separate doctrine). The memo runs them together. Also: imprescriptibility of an action to declare a void contract void does not automatically extend to reconveyance, which has its own prescriptive framework. Cause C (reconveyance) and Cause A (nullity) have different prescription analyses and the memo treats them as one.

**5. §V Defense 1 says "Cite the leading SC authority on void-source derivative titles" — literally a placeholder.**
A partner cannot sign over a memo that contains an instruction to cite a case. Either *Heirs of Gregorio Lopez v. DBP*, *Spouses Aguinaldo*, or whichever string-cite Barandon prefers needs to be in here. Same defect appears implicitly in Defense 2 (no IPV authority cited), Defense 3 (no laches/imprescriptibility authority).

**6. The SPA-vs-guardianship sequencing recommendation is internally contradictory.**
§V Defense 5 says "filing must be delayed until at least one authority is locked in." §VII targets "mid-to-late June 2026" filing. §IX recommends "do NOT file before the guardianship petition is at least filed and docketed" — but the guardianship petition itself has not been filed yet (§IX status: "Petition drafted; pre-filing review pending"). So the recommended trigger condition has no committed date. Either commit to SPA-only filing with the capacity risk explicitly accepted by the principal, or set a hard date by which guardianship must be filed. "Hybrid" as currently written is a non-decision.

**7. §IX names a "June 2 mediation" / "pre-mediation momentum" with zero setup.**
First mention of mediation in the entire memo is inside the sequencing analysis. If there is a June 2 mediation event, it belongs in §I status and §VII timeline, not as a parenthetical justification for Path X.

**8. Tier I defendants' theory is overstated.**
§III says Tier I "cannot raise the 2005 revocation defense because their transactions predate it; their liability flows from the structural defects." Correct — but that means Tier I is the **weakest** tier for plaintiffs evidentially (depends entirely on Layers 1 and 2, both of which require the 1991/92 SPAs in hand, which the memo admits we don't have). The tier introduction frames every tier as comparably strong; it isn't. Tier I is the most exposed if the SPAs cannot be produced. Say so.

**9. Cause H (public officer liability) is undercooked.**
The ARTA cases are name-dropped in §IV but no individual respondents are named, and §III Tier V leaves them as John Does 101-200. If the ARTA filings have already named specific officials, those names should migrate into the Tier V list. If they haven't, Cause H is essentially aspirational and the ₱5M exemplary damages estimate in §IV-Cause G is unanchored.

**10. §VI claims "Verification template ✓ Ready" but §V Defense 5 admits the SPAs may not all be executed.**
A verification cannot be ready when the underlying authority chain is incomplete. These are dependent items and the readiness checklist treats them as independent.

## (C) What Would Prevent a Partner From Putting Their Name on It

- The placeholder "Cite the leading SC authority" in §V Defense 1. This is a sign the memo was shipped before completion.
- The 35-40 vs. 26 derivative-title gap with no resolution plan inside the 30-day window.
- Cesar's death certificate being item #8 of 10 on the outstanding list while simultaneously being labeled "the cleanest factual fulcrum."
- The mediation reference appearing only inside §IX sequencing — a partner reading top-to-bottom will not know there is a calendared adverse event until p. 8.
