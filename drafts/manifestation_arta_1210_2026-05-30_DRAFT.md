## B. ADDITIONAL GROUNDS (extending the Pending Petition's theory to the Supervening Resolution)

### Statement of the Case (Supervening Resolution)

On 27 January 2026, Petitioner filed an Initial Complaint with ARTA against the Municipal Treasurer's Office and the Office of the Municipal Mayor of Mercedes for persistent failure to act on six (6) ministerial requests for real-property-tax information and for refusal to issue Official Receipts on a Php 75,000.00 payment under protest. On 5 March 2026, Petitioner submitted a Sworn Complaint-Affidavit (Annex "M-2"). The complaint was endorsed to the Mercedes CART. On 13 May 2026, ARTA issued the Supervening Resolution recommending CLOSURE for absence of prima facie evidence under Sec. 21(d) and (e) of R.A. No. 11032 — and saying nothing about the alleged Sec. 21(b) violation. Notice was received via email on 15 May 2026.

On **18 May 2026**, Petitioner filed before the ARTA Litigation Division a *Manifestation of Legal Position and Request for Written Clarification* (Annex "M-3"), expressly stating that it was not a motion for reconsideration and requesting written clarification on three specific points of law arising from the Supervening Resolution.

On **21 May 2026**, ARTA's Litigation Division (through Atty. Rodolfo B. Del Rosario Jr., Division Chief) issued a written *Response to Manifestation of Legal Position and Request for Written Clarification* (Annex "M-4"). The Response is critical to the present Manifestation because ARTA there expressly **narrowed and limited** the reach of the Supervening Resolution — disclaiming any determination of the LGU's substantive duties under the Local Government Code, disclaiming any determination of property-owner rights or remedies, and pointing Petitioner to "the appropriate supervisory authority, or other competent forum" for relief on those substantive matters. That supervisory authority is this Honorable Office.

### Statement of Strategy

Petitioner's threshold submission is that the Supervening Resolution rests on the same two legal errors already pleaded in the Pending Petition: a narrow reading of "government service" under Section 4(f), and a citizenship-based dismissal that conflates the constitutional right to information with the statutory protections of R.A. No. 11032. Those issues are fully briefed in the Pending Petition and should not need to be re-litigated in a separate appeal. A single OP ruling can — and should — dispose of both.

**Ground III** (failure to address Sec. 21(b)) is independently sufficient to set aside the Supervening Resolution on procedural / due-process grounds, regardless of how the consolidated substantive issues are decided. It is unique to the Supervening Resolution and is the cleanest reversal lane available.

### Additional Grounds Specific to the Supervening Resolution

#### GROUND I — The Supervening Resolution applies the same narrow construction of "government service" already challenged in the Pending Petition.

The Supervening Resolution holds that Petitioner's requests are "requests for information and clarification of tax records" and therefore not "applications for a permit, license, clearance, concession, privilege, or authorization contemplated under Section 4(f) of R.A. No. 11032." This is the identical construction challenged in the Pending Petition, raised here for the limited purpose of demonstrating that a unified ruling can dispose of the issue across both Resolutions.

1. Section 4(f) defines "government service" to include processes involving applications "for any privilege, right, reward, license, clearance, permit or authorization, concession, or for any **modification, renewal, or extension thereof**, which are acted upon in the ordinary course of business." Real Property Tax assessment is itself such a transaction; the taxpayer is asserting a *right* to be correctly assessed and to pay the lawful tax (including the 20% early-payment discount under Sec. 251 LGC).

2. Section 21(b) of R.A. No. 11032 — penalizing refusal to issue Official Receipts — is itself textual proof that "official receipts" sit within the scope of the statute, and an Official Receipt is by nature issued in a tax-payment transaction, not in a license/permit application. ARTA's narrow reading would render Section 21(b) without field of application.

3. The "3-7-20 rule" under Section 9 of R.A. No. 11032 applies to "all government transactions" — not merely those involving a permit or license. The Supervening Resolution silently introduces a textual qualifier the legislature did not write.

#### GROUND II — The Supervening Resolution applies the same citizenship-based dismissal already challenged in the Pending Petition.

The Supervening Resolution disposes of the case on the holding that because Petitioner and Patricia Keesey Zschoche are American citizens, "the request cannot be treated as an application for a legally demandable right to information under the cited constitutional and executive issuances." This is the same conflation challenged in the Pending Petition.

1. Section 7, Article III, 1987 Constitution and E.O. No. 2, s. 2016 are citizen-limited rights of access to information. Petitioner does not invoke them as the basis for relief.

2. The complaint invokes R.A. No. 11032, which contains no citizenship qualifier. Its protections run in favor of "applicants or requesting parties" — language that is intentionally citizenship-neutral.

3. The property at issue — TCT T-4497 and its derivative titles in Mercedes — is Philippine-titled real property owned of record by Filipino registrants. The RPT obligation arises from the Philippine property itself, not from any privilege held by the heirs. The MTO's ministerial duties run in favor of the property's tax account, not in favor of any particular nationality of heir.

4. Petitioner acts under a duly-notarized Special Power of Attorney as Attorney-in-Fact for Patricia Keesey Zschoche, an heir, in respect of a Philippine real-property tax matter. The ministerial duty of an MTO does not turn on the personal citizenship of the taxpayer's representative when that representative is dealing with Philippine real property under Philippine title.

5. ARTA's contrary reading would produce the absurd result that any foreign-owned property in the Philippines would have no administrative remedy for LGU red-tape, contrary to the broad policy purposes of R.A. No. 11032.

#### GROUND III — The Supervening Resolution failed to address a specifically alleged violation of Section 21(b) (refusal to issue Official Receipts). [UNIQUE TO THE SUPERVENING RESOLUTION]

The Complaint-Affidavit specifically alleged a Section 21(b) violation in respect of the Php 75,000.00 paid on 19 December 2025 under Confirmation Nos. VCHMU47RH and VYFLCEPVH. The Supervening Resolution's RECOMMENDATIONS dispose only of "Section 21(d) and (e)" — silent on Section 21(b).

1. The refusal to issue an OR for an actual remittance under verified Confirmation Numbers is a textbook ministerial violation independent of any information-disclosure question. The taxpayer has the right under Sec. 252 LGC to pay under protest and to receive an OR annotated "PAID UNDER PROTEST," which is a statutorily-mandated act subject to no discretion.

2. The Resolution's failure to address this allegation either constitutes (a) a denial of due process to Petitioner on a specifically pleaded ground, or (b) an implied dismissal without articulated reasoning, which would itself be reversible error.

3. **This ground is independent of the consolidated substantive issues.** Whatever this Honorable Office rules on the citizenship and scope questions, the Supervening Resolution is independently subject to reversal for failing to address Section 21(b).

#### GROUND IV — The Supervening Resolution's interpretation contradicts the policy and purposive intent of R.A. No. 11032.

R.A. No. 11032 was enacted to "promote integrity, accountability … efficient turnaround of the delivery of government services and the prevention of graft and corruption." The Resolution's reading would judicially excise from this remedial statute any LGU dealing with foreign-citizen heirs of Filipino-titled property — a class neither anticipated nor warranted by the legislature, and now arising in two separate ARTA Resolutions against the same LGU.

#### GROUND V — ARTA's own clarification letter of 21 May 2026 confines the Supervening Resolution to a jurisdictional / prima facie finding, and expressly points to this Honorable Office as the proper forum for the substantive issues. [UNIQUE TO THE SUPERVENING RESOLUTION]

In its 21 May 2026 *Response to Manifestation of Legal Position and Request for Written Clarification* (Annex M-4, doc#972 in the case record), the ARTA Litigation Division — through Division Chief Atty. Rodolfo B. Del Rosario Jr. — narrows the Supervening Resolution in four respects that are directly material to the present appeal:

1. **ARTA disclaims any adjudication of the LGU's substantive duty.** "[T]he Resolution did not adjudicate ownership rights, real property tax liabilities, the validity of payments made, or the propriety of any action or inaction of the Municipal Treasurer's Office under the Local Government Code." (Del Rosario Letter, page 1, emphasis added.) The Supervening Resolution is, by ARTA's own characterization, jurisdictional and prima facie only.

2. **ARTA disclaims any determination on citizenship as a substantive bar.** "[T]he reference in the Resolution to Section 7, Article III of the 1987 Constitution and Executive Order No. 2, series of 2016, was made in relation to the nature of the requests as framed and supported in the complaint, i.e., as requests for access to official records and information. Such discussion was not intended to conclusively determine the full extent of any property owner's rights or remedies under the Local Government Code, nor to foreclose the complainant from pursuing appropriate relief before the proper office, agency, or tribunal." (Del Rosario Letter, page 2, emphasis added.)

3. **ARTA expressly disclaims any holding that the LGU may refuse to issue receipts.** "Stated differently, the Resolution should not be read as a determination that the Municipal Treasurer's Office may lawfully refuse, withhold, or deny any document, receipt, or record to which a taxpayer or registered property owner may otherwise be entitled under applicable law." (Del Rosario Letter, page 2.)

4. **ARTA expressly directs Petitioner to "the appropriate supervisory authority."** "Those matters involve substantive questions concerning local taxation, property records, and the administration of real property tax payments, which are **more properly addressed to the concerned local government unit, the appropriate supervisory authority, or other competent forum**." (Del Rosario Letter, page 2, emphasis added.) For an LGU of Mercedes, Camarines Norte, that "appropriate supervisory authority" is — through the chain of executive supervision over local government units — this Honorable Office.

The combined effect is that ARTA itself has cleared the path for this Honorable Office to reach the substantive questions the Supervening Resolution did not decide:

   (a) Whether the LGU's refusal to issue Official Receipts (annotated "PAID UNDER PROTEST") for the Php 75,000.00 paid on 19 December 2025 under Confirmation Nos. VCHMU47RH and VYFLCEPVH violates Section 252 of the Local Government Code and Section 21(b) of R.A. No. 11032;

   (b) Whether the LGU's refusal to disclose assessment records pertaining to **TCT No. 48336** (registered in the names of Geraldine K. Hoppe, Patricia K. Zschoche, and Marcia Ellen Keesey) violates Section 276 of the Local Government Code; and

   (c) Whether Section 7, Article III, 1987 Constitution and E.O. No. 2, s. 2016 — which restrict the constitutional FOI right to Filipino citizens — have any application to a registered co-owner's request, through duly authorized Attorney-in-Fact, for assessment records pertaining to her own titled property.

ARTA has expressly answered "not us" to each. The Local Government Code and R.A. No. 11032 do not leave a remedial gap. This Honorable Office is the proper supervisory authority, as ARTA itself has indicated.
