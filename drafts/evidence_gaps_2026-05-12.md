## II. WHY THIS MATTERS LEGALLY

PD 1529 (Property Registration Decree) §53 is unambiguous: no voluntary
instrument shall be registered "until the same has been properly notarized
and the corresponding fees paid". Without the Deed of Sale itself, the
Owner's Duplicate Copy, and the BIR CAR, the Register of Deeds has no
basis to cancel the prior title and issue a new one — and the resulting
TCT would be void on its face under Cabotaje v. Spouses Pudunan
(G.R. 161892) and the jurisprudence on void Torrens titles obtained
through forged or fraudulent transfer.

For Civil Case 26-360, the universe of relief is therefore broad: if the
demand letter and complaint can show on the face of public records that
**none of the required transfer instruments exist** for the chain that
produced T-079-2021002126 (the contested defendant title), then the
*accion reinvindicatoria* converges with a claim of void title — which
under Spouses Galang v. Reyes-Galang (G.R. 197051) does not require
proof of fraudulent intent, only the absence of a valid root.
