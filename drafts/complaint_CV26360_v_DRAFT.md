# COMPLAINT

## I. PREFATORY STATEMENT

This case seeks to unwind more than three decades of unauthorized subdivisions, sales, and transfers of property originally covered by Transfer Certificate of Title No. T-4497, committed by Cesar M. De La Fuente, whose legal authority was (1) limited from the beginning under the 1991 and 1992 SPAs, (2) expressly and impliedly revoked as early as 1999, (3) formally revoked in 2005, and (4) extinguished by his death in 2017.

Yet despite lacking authority, he continued, and others continued after him, to execute, register, or benefit from void transactions affecting approximately 35–40 parcels, resulting in multiple derivative titles that cannot legally stand under the Civil Code, P.D. 1529, and controlling jurisprudence.

Plaintiffs now seek judicial declaration that: the acts of an unauthorized agent are void ab initio; all derivative TCTs must be cancelled; TCT No. T-4497 must be reinstated undivided; possession and fruits must be restored; defendants claiming through void acts must yield title, possession, and income.

No amount of registration or passage of time can breathe life into void transactions. Fraud, concealment, and lack of notice prevent laches from applying.

## III. JURISDICTION AND VENUE

This Honorable Court has jurisdiction under B.P. 129, as amended by R.A. 11576, because the assessed value of the properties involved exceeds ₱10,000,000. Venue is proper in Daet, Camarines Norte because the property is located within this Court's territorial jurisdiction; the cause of action concerns title and possession of real property; and several defendants reside in the province.

## IV. THE SUBJECT ESTATE

Plaintiffs are the lawful heirs of Mary Worrick Keesey, registered owner of large tracts of land originally embraced under Transfer Certificate of Title No. T-4497 ("the primitive title"). A certified true copy is attached as Exhibit B.

TCT No. T-4497 covers multiple agricultural and residential parcels situated in the Municipalities of Daet and Mercedes, Camarines Norte, whose collective area exceeds hundreds of hectares, and whose assessed values exceed ₱10,000,000.

Upon Mary's death in 1988, her daughters—the plaintiffs herein—became co-owners of the entire estate in *pro indiviso* shares pursuant to Articles 777 and 493 of the Civil Code. The estate remained undivided. No judicial or extrajudicial partition was ever carried out. Thus, no person—including any purported agent—could lawfully alienate, segregate, transfer, or subdivide specific and determinate portions of the land.

Despite the indivisible status of the estate, numerous derivative TCTs were issued over the years based on unannotated, unauthorized, and legally impossible transactions, resulting in approximately 35–40 questionable titles, listed in **Annex "A"**.

## V. FACTUAL ANTECEDENTS

### A. Origin of Ownership
TCT No. T-4497 serves as the indefeasible root title under the Torrens system. The property remained whole, unfragmented, and under the name of Mary Worrick Keesey for decades. The heirs never authorized any sale or transfer of any portion of the land, nor did they consent to any subdivision thereof.

### B. Co-Ownership and Indivisibility
Because the estate was inherited without partition, the heirs' shares remained ideal and undivided, not delineated into specific, metes-and-bounds portions. Any sale, subdivision, or transfer of specific physical portions of co-owned property before partition is void *ab initio*. This principle governs the entire case: no agent or transferee could have acquired anything more than a void expectation.

### C. The 1991 and 1992 Special Powers of Attorney (SPAs): Narrow and Limited
In **1991** and again in **1992**, plaintiffs executed Special Powers of Attorney (SPAs) appointing Cesar M. De La Fuente for narrow purposes, typically: to "facilitate" or "negotiate" certain pre-existing contractual interests of one Ben Llamanzares, subject to consultation, subject to the obligation to account, with **no express authority to sell,** and **no authority whatsoever to subdivide** the estate. Subdivision is a distinct and extraordinary act requiring express and specific authorization, never implied. Thus, from inception, and under both the 1991 and 1992 instruments, Cesar had no power to fragment the land nor to convey ownership of specific parcels.

### D. Unauthorized Subdivisions and Sales Begin in 1991
Evidence shows that immediately following the execution of the 1991 SPA, Cesar had already begun issuing deeds, facilitating surveys, and engaging in transactions over precise portions of TCT 4497. These acts were void not only for lack of authority under the defective SPAs, but also because they constituted premature unilateral partition of a co-owned estate, expressly prohibited by Article 493. No annotation existed on TCT 4497 for any of these acts.

### E. The Scheme Expands (1991–1999): 20+ Void Sales and Undocumented PSD Plans
Between 1991 and 1999, Cesar carried out dozens of void transactions, using similar patterns: preparation of unannotated deeds; participation in "subdivision" surveys with no authority; presentation of himself as owner or authorized seller; failure to remit or account for any proceeds; and concealment of transactions by ensuring no encumbrance annotations on the mother title. Many derivative titles trace their lineage to this period of unauthorized acts.

### F. The 1999 Revocation: Express, Implied, and Legally Effective Despite Lost Proof of Delivery
In 1999, the heirs prepared and dispatched a revocation letter terminating Cesar's authority. From 1999 onward, plaintiffs had no contact whatsoever with Cesar, nor did he render any accounting. His non-accounting and prolonged cessation of contact automatically extinguished whatever limited agency he once held, making his authority dead for all legal purposes.

### G. The 2005 SPA Revocation: Formal and Unconditional Termination
In 2005, plaintiffs issued a formal, explicit revocation of all authority previously granted to Cesar under the 1991 and 1992 SPAs. This revocation is undisputed and terminates any residual or arguable authority. Thus, all acts from 2005 onward are unquestionably void.

### H. The 2007 Public Notice and Post-2005 Acts: Proof of Bad Faith
Despite the 2005 formal revocation, Cesar continued his scheme. To protect their interests, Plaintiffs published a **Public Notice in 2007** regarding the irregularities concerning the estate and Cesar's lack of authority. This notice provides **constructive notice** to all persons dealing with the property thereafter. All transactions and transfers executed by Cesar after the 2005 revocation, and certainly after the 2007 public notice, are not only void for lack of agency but also fatally defective due to the buyers' inability to claim good faith.

### I. The 2017 Death of Cesar Extinguishes Agency Absolutely
In 2017, Cesar died. Under Article 1919(3), his death completely extinguished any residual or theoretical agency. Astonishingly, transactions and title issuances continued after his death, rendering them legally impossible. Any title whose chain depends on acts performed after the agent's death is void many times over.

### J. Fraud Concealment and the Discovery Rule: Why Laches Cannot Apply
Plaintiffs resided abroad, and there was no annotation of any deed, SPA, revocation, subdivision plan, or encumbrance on TCT 4497 that could have placed them on notice. Fraud actively concealed cannot give rise to laches. Because the underlying acts are void, not merely voidable, the action to declare nullity does not prescribe.

### K. Discovery of the Fraud (2020–Present)
Plaintiffs discovered the fraudulent transactions only in 2020. Plaintiffs immediately issued Public Notices of Nullity (including a reaffirmation of the 2007 notice), Requests for records from the ROD, Adverse Claims, and Formal Demands, confirming their diligence and good faith, defeating any allegation of negligence.

### L. COMPLICITY OF GOVERNMENT EMPLOYEES AND OFFICES
Plaintiffs allege that certain employees and officials within the Registrar of Deeds (ROD) and the City/Municipal Assessor's Offices, acting under the category of **John Does 101–200**, performed their duties with **gross negligence or malicious intent** in processing the fraudulent transactions. Specifically, these officials: (1) Processed and issued derivative TCTs and Tax Declarations despite the patent and structural defect of **co-ownership and lack of partition** on TCT T-4497; (2) Processed documents and issued titles based on acts executed **after Cesar M. De La Fuente's death** (2017), a defect that is patent on its face; and (3) Knowingly allowed the issuance of building permits and property clearances based on these void titles, actively facilitating physical encroachment, and compounding the damages sustained by the Plaintiffs. These acts remove their official conduct from the sphere of ministerial duty and render them liable for damages.

## VI. CAUSES OF ACTION

The foregoing allegations are repleaded and incorporated herein by reference as if fully set forth.

### A. FIRST CAUSE OF ACTION: Declaration of Nullity of All Unauthorized Deeds, Subdivision Plans, and Transfers
A contract entered into by a person without authority to bind the principal is void *ab initio* and produces no legal effect. The 1991 and 1992 SPAs did not authorize subdivision or sale. Furthermore, every transaction executed after the 1999 implied revocation, the 2005 formal revocation, and the 2017 death of the agent is void by operation of law. Thus, the Court must declare all deeds, surveys, transfers, and derivative titles derived from Cesar's actions null and void *ab initio*.

### B. SECOND CAUSE OF ACTION: Cancellation of Derivative TCTs Under P.D. 1529
Derivative titles stemming from void documents cannot ripen into valid title. The "spring cannot rise higher than its source." This Complaint is a direct attack on multiple derivative certificates, which are fatally defective because the mother title (T-4497) was never lawfully fragmented. When the root of the title is void, indefeasibility does not attach, and all derivative TCTs must be cancelled.

### C. THIRD CAUSE OF ACTION: Reconveyance / Constructive Trust
Persons who acquire property by mistake, fraud, or unauthorized transactions become trustees of an implied constructive trust for the benefit of the true owner. Every defendant who holds a portion of TCT 4497 as a result of Cesar's void acts is an involuntary trustee obligated to reconvey possession and title.

### D. FOURTH CAUSE OF ACTION: Accion Reivindicatoria (Recovery of Ownership and Possession)
Plaintiffs, as true owners of the undivided estate covered by TCT 4497, have the right to recover ownership, possession, access, and enjoyment of fruits. Defendants' occupation or use of any portion of the property without lawful title constitutes unlawful withholding of property.

### E. FIFTH CAUSE OF ACTION: Quieting of Title
Plaintiffs seek judicial confirmation that they are the lawful co-owners of the entire estate, with TCT 4497 reinstated as the sole and controlling title. Clouds on title include all derivative TCTs, unannotated deeds, and unauthorized subdivision plans.

### F. SIXTH CAUSE OF ACTION: Accounting of Fruits, Rents, and Income
Occupants in bad faith are liable for natural and civil fruits enjoyed, rental value, and profits gained. Since defendants' possession stems from void deeds, they are possessors in bad faith and obligated to account for and return fruits from 1991 to present.

### G. SEVENTH CAUSE OF ACTION: Damages
Plaintiffs pray for: **Moral Damages:** ₱10,000,000.00 for decades of loss and violation of property rights due to concealed fraud. **Exemplary Damages:** ₱5,000,000.00 to deter repetition of land-grabbing schemes. **Attorney's Fees and Litigation Expenses:** ₱2,000,000.00 for reimbursement of expenses, plus ₱10,000.00 per hearing as appearance fees.

### H. EIGHTH CAUSE OF ACTION: Damages Against Public Officers for Malfeasance
(Arts. 27, 32, 2176, Civil Code) The knowing and malicious refusal or neglect by employees of the ROD and the Assessor's Offices to perform their official duties in good faith, as evidenced by their continued processing of patently void transactions (such as post-mortem deeds and subdivision of unpartitioned land), renders them personally and solidarily liable for damages under Articles 27 and 32 of the Civil Code. Their actions constituted a conspiracy of fraud which enabled the entire scheme to persist for decades, impairing the Plaintiffs' constitutional right to property and inflicting undue suffering.

### I. NO BAR OF PRESCRIPTION OR LACHES
When the foundational act is void, the action to declare nullity does not prescribe. Laches does not apply where there is no knowledge and no duty to know, especially where fraud was structurally hidden by the absence of ROD annotations and post-revocation acts.
