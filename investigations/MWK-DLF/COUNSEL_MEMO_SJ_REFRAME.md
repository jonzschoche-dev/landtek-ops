# Counsel memo — reframe the SJ argument before the hearing (Civil Case 26-360)

> **DRAFT for Atty. Barandon via Jonathan — internal work product, human-gated, not for filing as-is.**
> Compiled 2026-07-18 from the war-room audit of the full filed record (docs 393/405/407/408/409/414/415/424/441).
> Every assertion below carries a doc-cite; nothing beyond the verified record.

**One sentence:** our filed SJ motion leads with the one version of the void-sale theory the SPA's own
text defeats ("negotiate, not sell"), while the winning frame — the sale power existed but was
**class-limited to Llamanzares contract-to-sell / down-payment holders, a class Balane admits she is
outside** — is already proven largely by the *defendants' own exhibits*; the argument must be
reframed at or before the SJ hearing, and Jonathan's testimony aligned to it.

## 1. The problem (as filed)
- SJ motion (doc:393 ¶5): the SPA "does not contain any express grant of authority to sell… but only
  to 'negotiate.'"
- But SPA para 7 — quoted in **our own** JA (doc:441 Q23) — authorizes Cesar to "negotiate for the
  sale … in favor of third persons in possession of CONTRACTS TO SELL executed by … MR. BEN
  LLAMANZARES, and/or to those who have paid their down-payments … **and to sign and execute the
  necessary documents … as well as to receive the proceeds**."
- The defense has already loaded this (Answer doc:405 ¶39: the SPA "read as a whole" allowed a sale).
  Leading with negotiate-only invites losing the motion on our own exhibit.

## 2. The winning frame (same relief, stronger ground)
**"Cesar could sell — but only to a closed class. Balane concedes she is outside it. A sale to a
stranger to the class is beyond the special power, hence void (Arts. 1874/1878/1879; special powers
strictly construed — Cosmic Lumber, G.R. 114311; Bautista-Spille)."**
Proven by the defense's own papers:
- Balane's JA: **no Llamanzares contract-to-sell** (admitted).
- Their receipts bundle (doc:414): **earliest is 20-Oct-2003** (Efren Balane, ₱1,900) — a decade
  after the 1992 SPA, none naming Llamanzares, none predating the SPA → she was not a
  "down-payment" holder within the SPA's meaning either. (This also verifies SJ ¶10.)
- Answer ¶8 admits Cesar executed the 2016 deed (doc:415) — the date sequence is undisputed.

**Backup prong (argue second, with honest limits):** authority was disowned 15-Aug-2005 (doc:441
Q28 Exhibit F; RD letter doc:79) and Cesar died before the 2021 confirmation. Limits we must not
overstate: the 2005 clause is a *generic disowning that does not name Cesar*; no document proves
2005 notice to him; annotation came only in 2021. Under the lead frame this doesn't matter — a sale
outside the class is void regardless of notice.

**Drop as an element (keep only as accounting):** "no consideration to the principal." The deed
recites the agent received ₱250,000; Salvador swears it went to the estate's taxes (doc:407 S24).
Non-remittance is an Art. 1891 accounting claim against the de la Fuentes — it does not void the
sale as to the buyer, and overclaiming it exposes Jonathan on cross.

## 4. Aug-12: the concession-and-pivot (rehearse)
- Expect: *"The SPA says he could 'sign and execute… and receive the proceeds' — that's a power to
  sell, isn't it?"* → **Concede the clause, pivot to the class**: "Yes — for buyers holding a
  Llamanzares contract or prior down-payments. Mrs. Balane had neither; her family's own receipts
  begin in 2003."
- Expect: *"Show me proof Cesar was told in 2005."* → Do **not** claim personal knowledge of notice
  no document backs. State what the record holds (the disowning SPA, the publication, the RD letter)
  and rest on the class point.
- Expect the equities story (*"20 years of silence while an innocent family built"*). → Law: laches
  cannot validate a void deed (Arts. 1409-1410). Narrative: the owners were abroad, acted through
  counsel, published upon learning sales continued (2020), annotated (2021), sued.
